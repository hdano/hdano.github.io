You have learned in music theory that the formula for the Major scale is:
T-T-S-T-T-T-S and the formula for the Minor scale is:
T-S-T-T-S-T-T

Major Scale

Using the formula, you can get the major scale of C:
C – D – E – F – G – A – B – C

Notice that this scale starts at the second string (A) because that is where the lowest C note is closest to the nut. You can also play the lowest C note on the first string starting on the 8th fret

These are just major scale for C. Try to find the major scales for other keys.

Minor Scale

Using the formula, you can get the minor scale of A: (which is related to C)
A – B – C – D – E – F – G – A

This scale starts with the second string A open. Starting the A minor scale in the first string is also possible:
