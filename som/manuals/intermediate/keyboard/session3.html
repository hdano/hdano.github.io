Chord Blocks

This means playing all notes of a chord simultaneously forming a block.

Quarter Note Pattern

The left hand is playing chord block for each quarter note while the left hand is playing chord block at the start of a bar or if the chord changes. You can also play the quarter note with left hand. This can be used for slow pop and rock songs such as “Still” by Hillsong

Eight Note Pattern

This is the same as quarter note pattern but this time the right hand is playing eight notes. This can be used for fast rock songs such as “Happy Days” by Jesus Culture.

Syncopated Pattern

Both hands have syncopated rhythms. We will explain syncopation in the advanced class but for now, learn this pattern by ear. This can be used for pop and rock fast songs such as “By Your Side” by Hillsong.

Major Chord

We learned that a major chord, or major triad, is built by adding the 3rd and 5th notes of the major scale to the root note. So, you can get the C major chord by playing C – E – G all across the keyboard.

Try to figure out and play other major chords.

Minor Chord

We also learned that a minor chord, or minor triad, is built by adding the 3rd and 5th notes of the minor scale to the root note. So, you can get the C minor chord by playing C – Eb – G all across the keyboard.

Try to figure out and play other major chords.

Chord inversion is important while playing keyboard because not only it gives better melody to your chords but it helps you (or at least your right hand) to easily jump from one chord to another.

For example, C major chord has the following inversions:

Root position C – E – G
1st Inversion E – G – C
2nd Inversion G – C – E
